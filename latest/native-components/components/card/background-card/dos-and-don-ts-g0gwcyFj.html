Background Card

Background cards are used to display information geared towards discovery and inspiration. They should focus on sparking curiosity and guiding users to explore further.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Image

A background card only supports an image as its foundation and layers content on top. The image should be relevant to the title of the card.

 

Image scrim

A scrim is required over the background image to ensure content legibility. The scrim size is automatically determined based on the image ratio.

 

Badge

The Traveler’s Choice badge is fixed in the top-right corner of destination background cards and appears only on destinations that have been awarded.

 

Content area

The content area supports an eyebrow, title, and supporting text, providing a clear hierarchy of information. The eyebrow is typically used for metadata or category labels and the supporting text offers additional details or descriptions.

 

Button

Background cards can have an optional button to offer a clear and explicit action. In these cases, the button provides stronger affordance for the action and avoids ambiguity about where the tap target lies.

 

 

 

Behavior

 

Tap target: Whole card

The whole card should be tappable and bring user to the following detail screen. Keep the card simple and focused on a single topic with a single destination.

 

Explicit tap target

When a background card contains a CTA, only the button responds to user interaction, while the rest of the card surface does not.

 

Text wrapping

By default, content starts to truncate after a certain number of lines as follows:

Stand-alone vs Carousel

The behavior of a vertical card will depend on its context. Cards can exist alone, in a feed, grid, or carousel.

 

Stand-alone

A stand-alone card is a full-width card that highlights content that deserves individual focus. It can appear on a screen alongside multiple other components, allowing it to integrate seamlessly into a variety of layouts.

Carousel

Carousels show a sneak peek of the next card to indicate more cards. Learn more about carousels.

 

 

 

Customization

Background card images can be changed by selecting the media container component, which houses the asset.

 

To change the image, drill into the component and select the media container component.

 

Copy and paste or upload your new photo in the right hand fill section.

 

 

 

Best practices

 

Actions

Do

Keep the background card content focused and without any additional actions.

Don't

Do not include any actions within background cards.

 

Image scrim

Do

Always keep the image scrim behind the content area.

Don't

Do not remove the image scrim.

 

Media

Do

Only place images as background card foundations.

Don't

Do not place maps, videos, or illustration within background cards.
