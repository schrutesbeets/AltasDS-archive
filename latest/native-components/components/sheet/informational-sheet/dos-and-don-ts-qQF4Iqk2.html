Informational Sheet

Informational sheets provide supplemental details relevant to the traveler’s current browsing context. They do not require input from travelers and do not submit any data.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Sheet title

A title is required for informational sheets. The title appears at top within the sheet header and should summarizes the sheet content.

 

Image

Informational bottoms sheets have optional images (16x9) within the content container.

 

Supplemental information

The content within informational bottom sheets are preconfigured to hold supplemental information. They don’t require input from the user and they don’t submit data.

 

Supplemental informational sheets can be activated by the following:

  • Inline tooltip icons
  • Tertiary buttons & Text links
  • TC award

 

Offer information

Informational sheets can also hold offer specific information. This configuration can show a max of two offers with corresponding legal copy.

 

Offer informational sheets can only be activated by tapping on a slim banner component.

 

CTA

Informational sheets can have optional CTAs (tertiary buttons). They support linkage to terms & conditions or learn mores.

 

 

 

Activation

 

Inline tooltip icons

{ [Property 1] description providing context, purpose, and/or intent in 2-4 sentences. }

 

 

Tertiary buttons & Text links

Low-emphasis calls to action like tertiary buttons or underlined text links that invite users to explore more information. When tapped, they open a info bottom sheet with expanded details or terms.

 

TC award

Tapping on a Traveler’s Choice or Best of the Best award will activate its corresponding bottom sheet providing additional information about our awards.

 

Slim banner (Braze)

Tapping on a Traveler’s Choice or Best of the Best award will activate its corresponding bottom sheet providing additional information about our awards.

 

 

 

Behavior

 

Dismissal

Bottom sheets can be dismissed by:

  • Tapping or clicking the dismiss/close icon
  • Tapping or clocking on the scrim
  • Swiping or dragging the bottom sheet down

 

Text wrapping

It’s recommended that the title of an informational sheet take up only one line of text (~30 to 35 characters). While the title will not truncate, it is best to keep it short and to the point.
The sheet content (sub copy) does not have a copy limit, but it’s best to keep copy to the point.

 

 

 

Best practices

 

CTA

Do

Only tertiary buttons can be utilized within informational bottom sheets.

Don't

Informational sheets do not support primary CTAs. Do not change the button type.

 

Imagery

Do

Images should not contain text or content messaging within it. Any visual elements should be exported within the image.

Don't

Do not include text within the bottom sheet image.
