Vertical Card

Vertical cards are used to display a summarized collection of news, content, or a POI.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Media

Media is required for all vertical card. Supported media include images and maps. Learn more about media container guidelines here.

 

Labels

Merchandising labels sit directly below the media and above the product title. It indicates key information about the POI and only appear one or two at a time.

 

Title

The card title should be the full name of the POI. The title wraps at three lines before being truncated.

 

Bubble Rating & Distance

Bubble rating is required for all cards highlighting a specific POI. Distance is optional.

 

Secondary content

Secondary content represents the core informational details of the item. This can include identifiers like descriptions, saves, and location etc. The content is customizable based on design needs, and each line can optionally include an icon.

 

Value props

Value props are short, descriptive phrases that highlight the unique benefits of a POI. They help users quickly understand what makes it valuable. Cards can have up to four lines of value props.

 

Value prop variants

Value props can display a range of content. Each BU has its own set of value props that can be toggled on or off as needed.

 

Price

Price is one of the most important and used custom elements within vertical cards. The price sits on its own line as the final information in the layer stack.

 

Price variants

Prices have a variety of variants across different cards. They support supplemental content as well as strikethroughs.

 

 

Chips

Action chips can be stacked within vertical cards. Their actions must be relevant to the content.

 

 

 

Behavior

 

Action

Each card has an action on the top-right of the image. The default action is a save button.

 

Additional actions

Additional actions are available such as an overflow icon button inline with the primary content. State layers including hover, pressed, and focused still behave the same.

 

Text wrapping

Card titles wrap at three lines before being truncated.

 

Tap target

The whole card should be tappable and bring user to the following detail screen. Keep the card simple and focused on a single topic with a single destination.

 

 

 

Feed vs Carousel

The behavior of a vertical card will depend on its context. Cards can exist alone, in a feed, grid, or carousel.

 

Feed

A feed is multiple vertical cards stacked in a long scrolling view. Cards in a feed view are full-width to allow more content.

Carousel

Carousels show a sneak peek of the next card to indicate more cards. Learn more about carousels.

 

 

 

Best practices

 

Hierarchy

Do

Keep the fixed tier of vertical cards in its original order.

Don't

Do not rearrange the order of the fixed tier.

 

Alignment

Do

Keep all content left aligned.

Don't

Do not custom align elements within the card.
