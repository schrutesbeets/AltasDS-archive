Histogram

Histogram displays the distribution of ratings and review counts, helping users quickly understand the overall feedback at a glance.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Rate label

Labels, which identifies the category, dimension, or rating level being represented, are always required and should be kept concise and descriptive, using consistent terminology across all histograms for clarity.

 

Bar & Track

The histogram bar is the filled portion that represents the rating value, scaling proportionally to show distribution or score. It is required within the histogram component when the rating value is above zero. Use consistent brand or rating colors for clarity and recognition.


The track serves as the background container for the histogram bar and defines the total possible range of the rating. It is required in order to give the bar context and should maintain sufficient contrast with the filled bar without overwhelming it.

 

Rating value

The rating value is the numerical display associated with the histogram bar. This may represent either a counT or an average score.

 

 

 

Usage

 

Traveler insights

Histograms are used to surface traveler insights by visualizing the distribution of ratings and reviews. Instead of only showing an average score, histograms break down how many travelers rated an experience as excellent, good, average, poor, or terrible.

 

 

 

Behavior

 

Empty/Zero state

If a label has no associated ratings, the histogram bar remains empty while the rating value displays “0”.

 

Minimum

The minimum width of the bar shrinks to a circle.

 

Responsive layout

The component adapts to different screen sizes and container widths. Bars and tracks scale horizontally, while labels and rating values maintain alignment and spacing.

 

 

 

Best practices

 

Orientation

Do

Always use a consistent histogram orientation.

Don't

Do not mix vertical and horizontal histogram items.
