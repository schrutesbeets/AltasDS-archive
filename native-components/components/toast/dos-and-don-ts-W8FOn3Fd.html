Toast

Toasts provide brief feedback to users after taking an action.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Leading content

Leading content is optional. Toasts can support an icon at a default size of 24px or an image size at 40px.

 

Message

The toast message is required and informs that something occurred. The message should be concise.

 

Action

Toasts can contain an optional single action. The CTA should be a verb, contain no more than two words, and relate directly to the action that was taken.

 

 

 

Behavior & Display

 

Text wrapping

Toast messages should be kept brief. It is recommended to keep copy between 1-2 lines and a maximum of 3 lines. Truncation is not allowed as the message is critical to the user.

 

Width

Toasts have a maximum width of 440px. On small screens they should span full width minus the screen’s margins.

 

Dismissal

Toasts remain on the screen until they are timed out or dismissed by the user. They can be dismissed by:

  • Swiping the toast away
  • Tapping the toast/CTA
  • Self-dismissal

 

Self-dismissal duration/Transition

Toasts remain on the screen until they are timed out or dismissed by the user. They can be dismissed by:

1 line of text

3500ms

2 lines of text

3500ms

3 lines of text

Screen sizes

 

Small

Toasts appear from the bottom of the screen on smaller/mobile screen sizes. There sit 8px above the global nav bar.

 

Medium

Toasts appear from the bottom right of the screen on medium/tablet screen sizes.

 

 

 

Best practices

 

Actions

Do

Use the text button for undoing actions, retrying an action, or allowing users to edit.

Don't

Do not utilize the text button to close out the toast as they disappear on their own. In these cases, do not include a button.

 

Follow-ups

Do

Follow up with another toast — If the toast action is to “undo” or an editable action is taken, it is good practice to follow up with a toast that confirms that action.

Don't

Do not fail to provide a confirmation after an undo or editable action. This may cause users to be uncertain whether their action was successfully completed.

 

One toast at a time

Do

Only show one toast at a time. If you need to show more than one, display the second after the first is dismissed. Queue them sequentially for better clarity.

Don't

Do not show more than one toast at a time. Showing more than one toast at a time can overwhelm users and make it difficult to focus on the most relevant message.
