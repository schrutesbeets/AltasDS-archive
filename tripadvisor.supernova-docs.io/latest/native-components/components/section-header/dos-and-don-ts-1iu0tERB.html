Section Header

Section headers separate sections and help organize the page into scannable chunks of content. They provide clear guideposts and bring users deeper into their content.

  • Overview
  • Dos & Don'ts

Properties

 

Eyebrow

An eyebrow is a short, optional line of text above the title. It’s used to provide context and guide attention before the main title.

 

Title

The title element is required to aid in organizing the content and establishing hierarchy. It should describe the content it is grouping.

 

Subtitle

A subtitle is optional. It is used to support the title in describing the content and its purpose.

 

Attribution

Attribution is optional in a section header and identifies the source or author of the content.

 

Trailing action

A trailing action is optional. This can be a tertiary button to show more results or an icon button, like a dropdown.

 

Partner logos

Partner logos can be displayed as trailing content within a section header. This placement provides clear visual association with the section while maintaining hierarchy.

 

 

 

Usage

 

Shelves

Section headers in shelves organize content into distinct, scannable groups. They help users quickly identify the purpose of each shelf and understand what content is included.

 

Hierarchy & Organization

Section headers establish visual and informational hierarchy across the interface. They guide users through content, making the structure and relationships between sections clear.

 

 

 

Behavior

 

Text wrapping

The title and subtitle in section headers should not truncate, as they are key way finding elements. Truncation can prevent users from understanding the section’s purpose at a glance. Instead, keep copy concise and within the recommended line count for each element.

Best practices

 

Title length

Do

Keep section headers short. Aim for a one to two lines at most.

Don't

Do not allow titles get too wordy. Simplify the title as much as possible, and add a 1-2 line subtitle if necessary.

 

Placement

Do

Place section headers at the top of the lists.

Don't

Do not place section headers above navigation headers.
