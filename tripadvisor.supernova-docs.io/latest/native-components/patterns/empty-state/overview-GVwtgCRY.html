Empty State

Empty states appear when expected content isn’t available or cannot be displayed. This can include first-time use of a feature, after completing or clearing tasks, when a search yields no results, or when an error prevents content from loading.

Guidelines

 

Communicate clearly

Clearly explain why the content isn’t available or why an error occurred. Use simple, jargon-free language so users immediately understand the situation without confusion.

Guide the user

Offer actionable steps for what the user can do next, whether it’s trying again, searching differently, or navigating to another part of the app. The goal is to keep them moving forward rather than feeling stuck.

Helpful and consistent

Maintain a supportive, non-blaming tone that aligns with the app’s personality. Use consistent language, visuals, and design patterns across empty and error states, and ensure accessibility for all users.

 

 

 

Anatomy & Appearance

 

Anatomy

Empty states may include an optional leading asset, such as a custom illustration. A headline with supporting subcopy is required to provide context, guiding users on what’s happening and what they can do next. A clear primary action should be included to help users recover from an error or explore the next step from an empty state.
