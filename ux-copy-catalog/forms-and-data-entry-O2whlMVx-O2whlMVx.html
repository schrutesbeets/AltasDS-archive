Forms & data entry

Used to capture users’ personal information in a contact form or a checkout page.

Fields

Variable, but always prioritize clarity and simplicity of language over brevity.

Ghost (shadow) copy

Offers clarity and instruction for users. Format can vary based on application, like: 

  • Listing examples: like adding (e.g., American, Italian, or Japanese) to a Cuisine type field for an Add a place form 
  • Defining how to use a space: like adding Add your comments in to an open entry field for a feedback form 
  • Illustrating the type of content requested: like adding a sample “tip” in the entry field for the Tips submission flow, such as “Espresso drinks at the cafe are free for guests 7-8 am, but it's not advertised.”

Use sparingly. Avoid use on common fields like email, phone, address, etc.

Buttons 

Keep short and action-oriented: the fewer words, the better. Use space to preview (and clarify) next steps for the user. Always default to sentence case, no punctuation.
